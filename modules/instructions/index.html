1. Basic Rules and Objective

Baccarat is a comparing card game played between two hands: the Player and the Banker. The objective of the game is simple—bet on which hand will have a total closest to 9. There are three possible outcomes for each round: the Player wins, the Banker wins, or it ends in a Tie.

At the beginning of each round, players place their bets on either the Player hand, the Banker hand, or the Tie. Once bets are placed, two cards are dealt to both the Player and Banker hands. Depending on the total value of the first two cards dealt, a third card may be drawn for either or both hands based on specific rules.

The hand with a total closest to 9 wins the round. If the Player and Banker hands have the same total, the round results in a Tie. The rules governing whether a third card is drawn are automatic, so players do not need to make any decisions beyond placing their initial bets.

Numbered Cards (2-9)

Worth their face value

Example: 5 = 5 points

10s and Face Cards

Worth 0 points

Worth 1 point

3. Table Layout and Equipment

Table Sections

  • Player Area: Designated spots for Player, Banker, or Tie bets
  • Banker and Player Boxes: Central areas where cards are placed
  • Tie Bet Box: Area for tie bets alongside main betting areas

Dealer's Area

  • Shoe: Holds multiple decks (6-8) of cards
  • Chip Rack: Stores casino chips for payouts
  • Discard Tray: Holds used cards
  • Commission Box: Tracks 5% commission on Banker wins

4. Dealing Procedures

  1. Opening the Round: Players place bets, dealer announces "no more bets"
  2. First Cards: Two cards dealt to both Player and Banker
  3. Natural Wins: Check for totals of 8 or 9
  4. Third Card: Draw according to rules if needed
  5. Determine Winner: Compare final totals
  6. Payouts: Process winning bets and collect losing bets

5. Third Card Rules

Player Hand Rules

  • Total of 0-5: Draw a third card
  • Total of 6-7: Stand
  • Total of 8-9: Natural - no draw

Banker Hand Rules

When Player DOES NOT take a third card:

  • Total of 0-5: Banker draws a third card
  • Total of 6-7: Banker stands
  • Total of 8-9: Natural - no draw

When Player DOES take a third card:

  • Total of 0-2: Always draw
  • Total of 3: Draw unless Player's third card is 8
  • Total of 4: Draw if Player's third card is 2-7
  • Total of 5: Draw if Player's third card is 4-7
  • Total of 6: Draw if Player's third card is 6-7
  • Total of 7: Stand

7. Common Variations

Punto Banco

Standard North American version with fixed drawing rules

Chemin de Fer

French version where players take turns as Banker

Mini-Baccarat

Smaller table, lower limits, faster pace

EZ Baccarat

No commission on Banker bets
